Callum Evans
Computer Science student

I am currently in the 4th and final year of my MSci in Computer Science at the University of Exeter and plan on continuing my studies in 2022 with a PhD, hopefully in France. Throughout my time at university, I have gained experience in many different fields within Computer Science, such as software development, artificial intelligence and machine learning, however, I’m mainly interested in urban informatics and its applications within environmental science.

I decided I wanted to research more into the topic when I had the opportunity to research the analysis of spatial data with a literature review surrounding the concentration of crime in cities, where I learned a great deal about data analysis methods. As I became even more interested in urban science, I then chose to create an urban digital twin for my 3rd-year dissertation, with a focus on traffic and emissions simulations. The project had the main goal of showing how digital twins and data visualisation can be incredibly useful in conveying complex and data-rich problems, such as climate change, and did well towards this goal. The completed project, source code and final report can be found on GitHub.

The Manchester digital twin project and the internship I did under the EPSRC vacation internship scheme hugely reaffirmed my interest in further studying the combination of computer science and urban science at a PhD level. So, for my final year project at Exeter, I decided I wanted to look at other applications of spatial data analysis and research gerrymandering and the ways machine learning can be used to draw electoral boundaries. All my main projects can be found in more detail here.

Outside of study, I also love sailing and I have been learning French since the beginning of the COVID-19 pandemic after having stopped a few years ago. In December 2021, I then was able to pass the DALF C1 exam in French, which I hope should allow me to study for my PhD in France or work there after graduation.

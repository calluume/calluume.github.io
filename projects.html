Previous Projects

My main research interests centre around the combination of urban and Computer Science, particularly looking at the environmental impact of our cities, although I’m also interested in other fields such as nature-inspired applications of Computer Science, machine learning and web development. My GitHub profile can be found here and will expand as I develop more projects alongside my degree, but below is an outline of some of my more recent projects.

Gerrymandering research project

For my final year project at Exeter, I am currently researching gerrymandering, which is the process of drawing electoral boundaries to benefit certain parties. The project aims to propose a new reinforcement learning-based approach to drawing fair electoral boundaries, directly optimising for fairness through comparisons with proportional vote. The method will be applied to the UK, and will be used to look at the proposed 2023 constituency boundaries, and will be completed by the end of April 2022.

Ant Colony Optimisation

This is my most recent project and is looking at applying ant colony optimisation techniques to a 2D ant colony simulation. The project aims to research swarm intelligence and works by simulating ants moving around a board, looking for randomly placed food, with their movements influenced by pheromones laid by other ants. Over time, the movements of the colony should converge to optimal paths between their home and food.

The Manchester Digital Twin

This is my largest completed project to date and was done for my 3rd-year dissertation. The project consisted of creating a digital twin of central Manchester, focusing on traffic simulation and resulting emissions. This had the ultimate goal of showing how digital twins could be used to convey complex and data-rich problems and systems to even non-experts, in this case showing how traffic affects emissions. The completed project can be seen on GitHub or at manchester-dt.co.uk.
